    FAQs

    1. What is the function of the Time Tracker (TT) app?

      The TT app monitors and tracks the amount of time spent on various tasks or activities while also capturing screenshots of your screen.

    2. What if I am using dual screens?

      In a dual screen setup, screenshots are exclusively captured on the main display of the PC.

    3. What if I am using multiple desktop feature in Windows 11?

      While utilizing the multiple desktop feature in Windows 11, it is important to note that any of your active desktops displayed on your screen can be captured by the TT app.

    4. What are the minimum system and network requirements for using the TT app?

      • The TT app should be used with either a wired broadband connection or a WiFi connection.
      • The user’s computer should have at least 20% available RAM to ensure smooth operation of the TT app.
      • It is important to start the TT app only when connected to the internet.

    5. How do I report ‘missing hours’ from my Timesheet?

      • If you have any ‘missing hours’ in your Timesheet, please submit a ticket through the provided link to notify our IT team about the issue.
      • To initiate a thorough investigation of the issue and prevent any future problems, kindly allow our IT team to examine your system.
      • Following the inspection, our IT team will assess the files on our server to determine the feasibility of rolling back the missing hours.
      • Please note that providing evidence of work, such as Bundy Clock records or Screenshot of work is essential for the validation of your request to roll back the missing hours. Failure to furnish proof of work will render your rollback request invalid.

    6. Which version of the Time Tracker App should I use?

      • The TT app is accessible across multiple platforms, including Windows, Mac, and Linux.
      • For Windows, the app is available in 64-bit format.
      • For MAC, the app is available as a .dmg file.
      • For Linux, the app is available as a JAR file.
      • The latest version of the TT app is X.X.XX (Refer website)

    10. What should be done if the TT app becomes unresponsive or freezes?

      • Locate and select the TT app from the list of the running applications, then choose the ‘End Task’ option.Additionally, please ensure to check the ‘Background processes’ section in the Task Manager.

      • Double-check your 'Timesheet' for any missing hours or discrepancies.
      • Reach out to one of the IT staff members via Skype messaging for further assistance.

    14. What information regarding dates and times is displayed on the TT app?

      • In the TT app, the dates and times are presented in Eastern Time (ET), as follows:
      • The displayed information indicates the date and time at which the user launched the TT app.
      • By default, the displayed date and time correspond to when the user initiates the TT app timer. The color indicator for this state is gray. When the user stops the timer, the color indicator changes to orange. In cases where the TT app stops due to internet problems, idle time-outs, or time jumps, the color indicator updates to red.
      • The provided data represents the current date and time clock.

    15. What steps can be taken to verify both offline and online Timesheets?

      If you have concerns about the completeness of your record, you can cross-reference your offline and online Timesheets. Follow these steps to check your offline Timesheet:

      • Open the TT desktop app and locate the ‘Offline Timesheet’ button, positioned below the ‘Log Out’ button.
      • Click on the ‘Offline Timesheet’ button to open the ‘Locally Stored Timesheet’ dialog box.
      • Select the desired week-ending date that you wish to review from the available options.
      • Click the ‘Show’ button to display the offline Timesheet for the selected week-ending date.
      • Cross-check the offline Timesheet with the corresponding online Timesheet.

      To check your online Timesheet, follow these steps:

      • Go to https://timetracker.20four7va.co/ and log in using your credentials.
      • Navigate to the Reports menu and select Timesheets.
      • Adjust the Date field to correspond to the desired week ending you wish to check, then click the refresh icon to update the information
      • Scroll down to the ‘Weekly Timesheet’ section and review the recorded data.
      • Cross-reference this information with your offline Timesheet to ensure accuracy.

      By following these instructions, you can access and compare the online Timesheet from the Reports menu with your offline records, allowing you to double-check the accuracy and completeness of your time entries.

    16. What to do when ‘offline and online Timesheets’ do not match?

      After cross-checking the Timesheets and identifying logs that are not synced online, users can utilize the ‘Sync from here’ button within the ‘Locally Stored Timesheet’ dialog box to initiate an automatic sync/upload process for those specific logs.
